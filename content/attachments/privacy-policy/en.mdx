- Data Controller (Personal Data Controller) – Oliwer Klauze.
- Personal Data – any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person (data subject); an identifiable natural person is one who can be identified, directly or indirectly, in particular by reference to an identifier such as a name, identification number, location data, an online identifier, or to one or more factors specific to the physical, physiological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural, or social identity of that natural person.
- Processing – any operation or set of operations which is performed on personal data or on sets of personal data, whether or not by automated means, such as collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation or alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure by transmission, dissemination, or otherwise making available, alignment or combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.

## Principles of Personal Data Processing
<TextBlock>
1. The Controller undertakes technical and organizational measures to protect personal data from unlawful, particularly unauthorized access or use, as well as from accidental destruction, loss, or compromise of integrity.
2. To ensure the security of the processed personal data, the following security attributes are taken into account:
    - Confidentiality – protection of personal data from accidental disclosure to unauthorized persons;
    - Integrity – protection of personal data from unauthorized modification;
    - Availability – ensuring access to personal data if the need arises.
3. Every person working for or providing services to the Controller who has access to personal data has the appropriate authorization and is obligated to maintain confidentiality.
4. Personal data that the User provides to the Controller in connection with using the websites and other services of the Controller is encrypted and protected by an SSL certificate.
5. In connection with the processing of the User's personal data, the Controller follows these principles:
    - Lawfulness – the User's personal data is processed in compliance with the legal grounds arising from Articles 6, 9, and 10 of the GDPR;
    - Fairness and transparency – personal data is processed fairly and transparently for the User, particularly through proper fulfillment of informational obligations;
    - Purpose limitation – the User's personal data is collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes and is not further processed in a way incompatible with those purposes; however, further processing for archiving purposes in the public interest, for scientific or historical research purposes, or for statistical purposes is not considered incompatible with the original purposes;
    - Data minimization – the User's personal data is processed in a manner that is adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary in relation to the purposes for which it is processed;
    - Accuracy – the User's personal data must be accurate and, where necessary, kept up to date, thus personal data that is inaccurate in light of the purposes for which it is processed is promptly erased or rectified;
    - Storage limitation – the User's personal data must be kept in a form that permits identification of the User for no longer than is necessary for the purposes for which the data is processed, provided that the technical and organizational measures required under the GDPR to protect the User's rights and freedoms are implemented, and provided that the personal data may be stored for longer periods if it will be processed solely for archiving purposes in the public interest, for scientific or historical research purposes, or for statistical purposes;
    - Integrity and confidentiality – the User's personal data must be processed in a manner that ensures appropriate security of the data, including protection against unauthorized or unlawful processing and against accidental loss, destruction, or damage;
    - Availability – the User's personal data must be protected from destruction, and the operation of the websites and related services must ensure that personal data is available to authorized persons when needed or at the appropriate time.
</TextBlock>
